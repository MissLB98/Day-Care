the principle

Working at Red Cross Children’s Hospital for twenty seven years in the Occupational Therapy as an assistant inspired me to improve quality of life in children.

It was not just merely a job but a passion that grew stronger, making a difference in young lives by treating children with normal developmental delays and psycho-social development.

The age groups ranges from 0 – 12 years. The ongoing passion for children inspired me to invest in reaching out to children to experience and gain from my knowledge and skill I have obtained over the years.

I wanted to invest in the community that I pursue my vision to study further by completing a NQF Level 4 and 5. It is my responsibility reach out to a child and raise up a generation.

vision

The vision of starting an ECD Centre did not happen overnight as it took ± 6 years ago in planning. To envision it through research and planning deciding the best location and the need in the community.

The location is on the main road, accessible and commuter friendly. The centre is situated in the radius of social services, other businesses, library, pharmacy, doctors’ surgery, the police station, bus and taxi terminus/rank.

Most of the time both parents need to work in today’s society and need a centre to care for their children. Of these parents are employed at these businesses or government institutions. The ECD Centre are able to render a service and employment to the community at hand as the need arise elsewhere.

The ECD Centre will encourage and support the empowering individuals working for the centre. These individuals will have to have or the centre’s support to obtain the qualifications, characteristics and passion to work with children at the centre.

The ECD Centre require excellence in the service rendering to children at all times. The daily programmes provided will be to enrich and build each child’s character and abilities holistically, thriving through learning.

The programmes includes social, emotional, physical, morale or spiritual. Successfully failure can be reduced and immediate intervention can be detected and referred for early intervention. It is a home away from home to children to make them feel safe in a happy and relaxing environment.

The community in the area are family orientated. The ECD Centre will serve and work alongside parents needing its services at prescribed times. The centre will form a special bond/agreement with parents in case of any unforeseen emergencies or circumstances.

Parents should feel free to liaise with the centre regarding any concerns or matters at hand. Our doors will be open to accommodate parents in need as far as possible. Parents will participate in the utilisation of the successfulness of the centre.

rules and regulations

parents:

Please adhere to the following rules and regulations of the centre.

  • The centre is open from 6.30am to 6.30pm.
  • A non- refundable registration fee of R150.00 is payable.
  • The application, indemnity, rules and regulations forms needs to be completed and signed by parent/guardian.
  • Children must have their items clearly marked.
  • Pack an extra set of clothes for your child daily.
  • The centre will not be liable for toys brought into the centre.
  • Inform the centre of your child’s absence from the centre before 9am.
  • Parents collecting their child after 6.30pm will incur a penalty fee of R25.00 every 15 min thereof.
  • Fees to be paid by the 28th of each month, with one months’ notice in the event of child leaving.
  • All money is to be paid into the bank account, no cash will be dealt with at the centre.
  • Fees not paid on time will result in your child’s acceptance until fees are settled.
  • For safety reasons inform the centre when someone else is collecting your child.
  • The child’s message book to be sent everyday with your child, read and signed.
  • Attendance of meetings is compulsory.
